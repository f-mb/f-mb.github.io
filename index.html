20.10.2013

I am a legal scholar, technologist and creative. A LL.D. candidate at University of Montréal and Ph.D. in Law candidate at University of Aix-Marseille, I am research assistant at the Chair in e-Security and e-Business Law, co-Editor-in-Chief of Les chantiers de la recherche en droit, and Head of communications at TEDxAix.

As a legal scholar, my research focuses on Intellectual Property and Information Technology Law; with a special interest for Trade-marks, I aim to explain and rethink law in the digital era. If my researches consume most of my time, reading and sometimes writing academic materials, I used to be a developer and creative/publicist. I still use these skills to spread researches into legal and non-legal world; and also for ‘side’ projects. In a nutshell, I'm a lover of freedom and the Internet, who decided to enroll in Law instead of IT and communication, ‘learning’ that by myself.

I hold a Licence and a Maitrise in Business Law, and a Master in Intellectual Property and Information Technology Law from University of Aix-Marseille. I also hold a Certificate in Criminal Law, Criminology and Forensic Sciences and a Certificate in Legal and Moral Sciences. And, in Winter 2013, I was a Graduate Visiting Student in Intellectual Property at McGill University.

I live between Aix-en-Provence (South of France) and Montréal, QC.

OpenSource Frameworks

ForceBleue powered by WordPress core

ForceBleue is a portal framework for publishing blog post, papers, books and video by subjects and jurisdictions. I developed it, as Research Assistant at Chair in e-Security and e-Business Law, to power droitdu.net, an academic news portal on Information Technology Law in French-speaking jurisdictions. Specially designed for law-related content, it can be used in other fields.

ForceRouge powered by WordPress core

ForceRouge is a website framework for professor or research group, with news, books, talks, press etc. I currently develop it, as Research Assistant at Chair in e-Security and e-Business Law, for the next release of Gautrais.com.

Coquelicot powered by WordPress core

Coquelicot is a website framework for events and symposium with news, speakers and talks. I developed it, as Head of Communication at TEDxAix, to run the TEDx event website.

When stable, these frameworks will be made available for free on GitHub.

Zotero and Citation Style Language

As an academic, Zotero is a powerful tool I use a lot, but not suited for legal writing (specially in French). So, I help improve mapping of Zotero fields to legal needs.

I also launch CSLégal, a project to make available major legal style for reference-manager software. I published in Citation Style Language the two French-speaking legal styles used in Canada (McGill and Lluelles).
